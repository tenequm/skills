# Skill Card

## Description

impactful-writing applies research-backed clarity, structure, and engagement principles to writing and editing any text - social posts, articles, documentation, emails, and code-review comments - for anyone who wants readers to understand, remember, and act on their words.

This skill is ready for commercial and non-commercial use.

## Use Case

Writers, developers, and communicators drafting or revising content - Twitter/X posts, blog articles, README files, professional emails, PR descriptions, announcements - who want concrete techniques (sentence-length targets, hook patterns, revision checklists, content frameworks) instead of vague style advice.

## Known Risks and Mitigations

Risk: Applying engagement-optimization patterns (hooks, curiosity gaps, persuasion frameworks like AIDA/PAS) to marketing or outreach content can drift into manipulative or clickbait copy.

Mitigation: The skill grounds every technique in clarity and reader value; users should review drafts for accuracy of claims and keep specific numbers and facts truthful before publishing.

Risk: Aggressive word-count cutting and simplification (10-30% reduction targets, jargon replacement) can strip technically necessary precision from documentation or legal/compliance text.

Mitigation: A human reviews edited output against the original for lost meaning; the skill's own validation checklist (core message test, cut test) requires verifying nothing essential was removed.

Risk: Cited research figures (comprehension percentages, engagement statistics) are synthesized summaries and may not match current platform behavior.

Mitigation: Treat platform-specific numbers as heuristics, not guarantees; test real content performance rather than relying on the stated statistics.
